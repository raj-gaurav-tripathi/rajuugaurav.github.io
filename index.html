Hello, I'm Raj.

I am an undergraduate student in the BS-MS dual degree physics program at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Kolkata. My primary academic focus lies in theoretical physics, specifically early-universe cosmology, inflationary models, quantum field theory, and high-energy physics phenomenology.

I am passionate about connecting foundational physical concepts to advanced theoretical frameworks, continually developing my mathematical intuition to unravel the complexities of the universe.

About Me

My academic journey is driven by a deep fascination with the fundamental laws governing spacetime and matter. I utilize rigorous mathematical frameworks, alongside computational tools like Python and Mathematica, to run numerical simulations for complex physics equations.

I have had the privilege of conducting research projects that explore the very fabric of reality. Currently, I am undertaking my Master's thesis focusing on causal set theory and quantum gravity at the Raman Research Institute under the guidance of Professor Sumati Surya. Previously, I completed a semester-long cosmological research project under the supervision of Professor Koushik Dutta.

Outside of theoretical physics, I incorporate running and jogging into my daily routine, participating in club marathons to maintain a balanced and refreshed mind.

Research Interests

Early-Universe Cosmology

Investigating the dynamics of the universe moments after the Big Bang, focusing on inflationary models and the generation of primordial perturbations.

Quantum Gravity

Exploring foundational theories like causal set theory to bridge the gap between general relativity and quantum mechanics.

Quantum Field Theory

Studying high-energy physics phenomenology and the behavior of subatomic particles through advanced mathematical frameworks.
