Academic Biography

I am an undergraduate researcher currently completing my Master's year in the BS-MS dual degree physics program at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Kolkata. My primary research is anchored in theoretical physics, with a distinct focus on early-universe cosmology, inflationary models, and quantum gravity.

My academic philosophy centers on the rigorous application of advanced mathematical frameworks to unravel the fundamental laws governing spacetime. I heavily utilize computational tools, including Python and Mathematica, to run numerical simulations that bridge foundational physics concepts with complex theoretical phenomenology.

Beyond the chalkboard and terminal, I maintain a disciplined routine of running and participating in club marathons to cultivate the mental endurance required for deep theoretical work.

Research Focus

Early-Universe Cosmology

Investigating the dynamics of the universe immediately following the Big Bang. My specific interests lie in single-field slow-roll inflation dynamics and the theoretical implications for the generation of primordial perturbations.

Quantum Gravity

Exploring foundational frameworks designed to reconcile general relativity with quantum mechanics. Currently focusing on the mathematical structures and discrete spacetime geometries within causal set theory.

Quantum Field Theory

Studying high-energy physics phenomenology, the behavior of subatomic particles, and the mathematical beauty of gauge symmetries underlying the fundamental forces.

Projects & Experience

Causal Set Theory & Quantum Gravity

Master's Thesis | Raman Research Institute

Currently undertaking a full-year thesis project under the supervision of Prof. Sumati Surya. The research focuses on the foundational aspects of causal set theory as a rigorous approach to quantum gravity.

Cosmological Inflation Dynamics

Research Project | IISER Kolkata

Completed a semester-long cosmological research project supervised by Prof. Koushik Dutta. Simulated single-field slow-roll inflation dynamics using Python to analyze theoretical implications for the early universe.

Space Weather Forecasting

Magnetohydrodynamics Computation

Developed a theoretical framework and Python-based data analysis pipeline. Utilized signal processing techniques to calculate solar plasma travel times and forecast geomagnetic disturbances.

Delayed Choice Quantum Eraser

Independent Research
